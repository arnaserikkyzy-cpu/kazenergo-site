Roof as a power plant: how the technology works

Traditionally, rooftop clutter and leak risks limited solar installations, while the danger of vertical supports under extreme winds made rooftop wind harvesting impractical. KazEnergo Rooftop removes these constraints.

  • elevator shafts, ventilation ducts and other equipment are enclosed inside the superstructure without changing their structural position;
  • the top surface becomes flat and fully available for PV (side faces can be used when needed);
  • the volume between the original roof and the top surface captures wind from all directions;
  • as air channels narrow toward the center, the flow accelerates and is directed to a wind generator in a protective enclosure;
  • this expands the operating wind-speed range and enables safe, low-noise operation.

Efficiency and advantages

The solution removes core rooftop barriers and boosts urban renewable performance by using high-altitude wind flows without towers or vertical supports.

  • generation within existing urban infrastructure ‚Äî no land take, no new roads, no long transmission lines;
  • electricity is produced at the point of use: cover building demand, store energy or export surplus to the grid;
  • a continuous superstructure surface simplifies waterproofing and significantly reduces leak risk;
  • electromagnetic shielding from telecom antennas becomes easier, approvals are simpler, and resident trust increases;
  • higher asset value and reduced stress on the power system amid fast-growing demand.

Why it matters for cities

The solution embeds generation into architecture and turns rooftops into energy assets. Instead of expensive high towers, it uses a superstructure on existing buildings, while the perimeter side faces can act as a working zone for capturing high-altitude wind flows.

  • scales within urban development;
  • works for both new builds and existing buildings;
  • produces electricity directly at the point of consumption;
  • reduces pressure on the power system as demand grows.
