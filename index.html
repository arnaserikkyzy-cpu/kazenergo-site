Technology: a superstructure that frees the roof

Typical roofs are cluttered with lift shafts, ventilation and equipment, leaving little space for RES and increasing the risk of leaks. EcoDom solves this:

  • a light or solid superstructure is built above the existing roof;
  • all top and side surfaces become usable for solar panels and micro-wind turbines;
  • antennas and telecom equipment are lifted above the roof line and away from windows;
  • the original roof remains sealed ‚Äì no extra penetrations.

As a result, the building gains a new ‚Äútechnical level‚Äù with green energy and telecom infrastructure, while residents keep a safe, protected roof.

Project economics

Payback comes from several revenue streams:

  • electricity sales from rooftop solar and wind turbines;
  • lease payments from telecom operators for base station placement;
  • additional lease of integrated technical or service premises;
  • asset value uplift from green infrastructure.

For new developments the payback period can start from 2‚Äì3 years. For existing buildings ‚Äì typically 3‚Äì5 years, depending on electricity tariffs, grid connection costs and building parameters.
